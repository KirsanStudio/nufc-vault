Newcastle United

A Club Forged in the North East

Club History

Newcastle United Football Club was formed in 1892 following the merger of Newcastle East End and Newcastle West End. The club has since become one of the most recognisable institutions in English football, representing not just a city, but an entire region.

Newcastle enjoyed early success in the 1900s, winning multiple league titles and FA Cups, and later became known for attacking football during the Kevin Keegan era of the 1990s. While major silverware has often proved elusive in modern times, the club’s support has remained among the most passionate and loyal in the world.

St James’ Park

St James’ Park has been the home of Newcastle United since 1892. Situated in the heart of the city, it is one of the most iconic stadiums in English football, with a current capacity of over 52,000.

Known for its towering stands and intense atmosphere, the stadium has hosted European nights, FA Cup ties, and unforgettable Premier League encounters. On matchdays, it dominates the city skyline — a constant reminder of football’s place in Newcastle life.
